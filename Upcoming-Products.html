Space Structures

Space structures are a three dimensional structure constructed from interlocking struts in a geometric pattern. These structures are lightweight and rigid. Frames can be used to bridge large areas with few interior supports.

Many materials are used for space frames like steel timber. This steel material is used for the space frames to sustain and carry the load of tones. This type of steel is mostly used in long span structures that have to be maintained well.

High Rise Building

We can build a High-rise steel structure of any height as per project requirement and laws from 23 m to 150 m height. This multistory steel framing structure made off site by using beam joist cold form deck slab and steel columns/composite columns.

High-rise steel buildings have some great benefits:
  • High strength to volume/mass ratio.
  • Speedy construction.
  • As a comparison to RCC frame steel frames are lighter in weight.
  • Occupies less space resulting in large open office space, large auditorium and concert hall.

Light Gage Buildings

Light gauge steel buildings are the steel skeleton used for a small structure. That can be produced in the factory & assemble on site. Further, the structure is then clad using metal sheets or Magnesium/Gypsum boards & insulation depending on usage. The resulting structure is water earthquake resistant. Applications include partition walls, low-cost housing, security cabins, small sheds and more light gauge steel structures that are non-combustible.
